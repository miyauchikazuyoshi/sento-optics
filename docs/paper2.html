Electronic Delocalization and Surface Tension: From sp/d Uniformity in Solids to the (δnuc, δelec) Description of Liquids

Kazuyoshi Miyauchi (Independent researcher)

Published: 2026-04-09 · DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.19425541 · sento-optics project · Companion: Paper 1

PDF (English) PDF (日本語) Zenodo GitHub

Abstract

Why do metals with similar bulk densities have vastly different surface tensions? And why do all liquids — metallic or not — exhibit surface luster?

In Part I, using periodic DFT slab calculations on 14 metals, we show that the valence electron uniformity ratio nmid/nbulk cleanly separates sp metals (mean 0.96) from d metals (mean 0.38; t = 5.84, p = 0.00008). A dimer-level delocalization index δIPR predicts this ratio at r = 0.89 (15 elements), and maximally localized Wannier functions confirm the hierarchy independently.

However, δelec alone does not predict surface tension γ (r = −0.18), because γ is a property of liquids, where nuclei are also delocalized (δnuc > 0). In Part II, we propose that liquid interface properties require both variables: electronic delocalization δelec (cohesive strength) and nuclear delocalization δnuc (surface self-healing).

Structure

Significance

This paper bridges Paper 1 (δ × Deff for optical response) with surface tension, discovering that electronic delocalization alone is insufficient for liquid properties. The two-variable description (δnuc, δelec) offers a unified framework connecting optical response and thermodynamic interface properties through particle delocalization.
